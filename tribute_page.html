Subash Chandra Bose

"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

Subhas Chandra Bose was a prominent figure in India's struggle for independence from British rule. Known for his advocacy of radical approaches and his leadership of the Indian National Army (INA), Bose's life was marked by his unyielding patriotism and his vision for a free India. Here is a timeline highlighting key points in his life:

  • 1897: Born in Cuttack, Orissa (now Odisha), into an influential family, setting the stage for his future role in India's independence movement.
  • 1919: Completed his education in India before traveling to England to appear for the Indian Civil Services exam, showcasing his brilliant intellect and early signs of leadership.
  • 1921: Resigned from his civil service post in solidarity with the non-cooperation movement led by Mahatma Gandhi, marking the beginning of his active involvement in the fight against British rule.
  • 1920s-1930s: Rose through the ranks of the Indian National Congress, becoming a prominent leader known for his advocacy of complete and immediate independence for India, often clashing with the party's more moderate leaders.
  • 1938-1939: Served as the President of the Indian National Congress, during which time he pushed for a more aggressive stance against British rule.
  • 1941: Escaped from British surveillance in India and traveled to Germany and later to Japan, seeking international support for India's independence struggle.
  • 1942-1945: Played a crucial role in forming the Indian National Army (INA) with the support of Japan, leading efforts to fight against the British forces in India from the Southeast Asian front.
  • 1945: Presumed dead after a plane crash in Taiwan, though his death remains shrouded in mystery and subject to much speculation.
